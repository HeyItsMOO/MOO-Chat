Your assistant is fully configurable from the **Assistant** editor in the dashboard. Here's what each section does.

## Personality & knowledge

- **Persona** — a short description of who the assistant is and the tone it should use. Be specific: "a warm, plain-English assistant for a Brisbane plumbing business" beats "a helpful bot".
- **Knowledge base** — the facts the assistant is allowed to use: products, services, pricing guidance, hours, policies, and FAQs. Write it the way you'd brief a new front-desk hire.
- **Guardrails** — explicit rules. Common examples: never quote a binding price, always recommend contacting a human for complex cases, stay on-topic.

## Appearance

Match the widget to your brand:

- **Colors** — primary and accent colors.
- **Header** — title and subtitle shown at the top of the widget.
- **Welcome message** — the first thing visitors see.
- **Suggested questions** — clickable prompts that guide visitors to your best answers.
- **Position** — left or right side of the screen.

On paid plans you can also remove the "powered by" branding.

## Behavior & limits

- **Model tier** — higher plans unlock more capable models for richer answers.
- **Rate limit** — caps how many messages a single visitor can send per hour, protecting you from abuse.
- **Monthly quota** — each plan includes a set number of AI replies per month.

## Tips for great answers

1. Keep knowledge-base entries short and factual.
2. Add the questions you *don't* want answered to your guardrails.
3. Review real transcripts weekly and fold new questions back into the knowledge base.
