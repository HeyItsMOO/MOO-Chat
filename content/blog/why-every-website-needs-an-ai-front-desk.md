Your website is open 24/7, but the person who can answer questions usually isn't. A visitor lands at 9pm, can't find your return policy or whether you service their area, and quietly leaves. That silent bounce is one of the most expensive things on the modern web — and it's completely fixable.

## The gap between question and answer

Every unanswered question is a tiny moment of friction. Individually they feel harmless. In aggregate they're the difference between a site that converts and one that leaks visitors. The traditional fixes all have problems:

- **FAQ pages** put the burden on the visitor to search and read.
- **Contact forms** create a delay measured in hours or days.
- **Live chat** only works when a human is staffing it.

What people actually want is an instant, accurate answer — at the moment they're wondering.

## What an AI front desk does

Think of it like the front desk of a good hotel. It greets people, answers the routine questions immediately, and knows when to call someone over. A well-configured AI assistant:

1. **Answers from your own content** — products, policies, hours, FAQs — instead of guessing.
2. **Captures leads** when someone is ready to talk, right inside the conversation.
3. **Hands off to a human** for the moments that genuinely need one.

The key word is *configured*. A generic chatbot that hallucinates is worse than nothing. An assistant grounded in your knowledge base, with guardrails, is a genuine asset.

## Why now

Two things changed. AI models got good enough to answer naturally and stay on-topic, and the install got simple enough that any business can add one in minutes — a single line of code, no developers. The barrier that used to make this enterprise-only is gone.

You don't need a big project to try it. Start with your ten most common questions, point the assistant at your existing content, and watch what visitors actually ask. The transcripts alone will teach you more about your customers than a month of guesswork.
