Getting people to your website is hard and often expensive. Converting them once they arrive is where most of the value is won or lost. An AI assistant, set up well, is one of the highest-leverage conversion tools you can add — because it meets visitors at the exact moment of intent.

## Start with intent, not interruption

The worst chat experiences shove a popup in your face before you've read a word. The best ones wait, stay out of the way, and become useful the moment you have a question. Configure your welcome message and suggested questions around the things people actually want to know first: pricing, availability, "do you do X?"

## Make the ask natural

A lead form that appears out of nowhere feels like a toll booth. A lead form that appears *after* the assistant has helped someone feels like a natural next step. The sequence matters:

1. Answer the visitor's question genuinely.
2. Offer the next step ("want a quote?", "should we send details?").
3. Capture just enough to follow up — name, email, and one or two qualifying fields.

Every extra field you ask for costs you completions. Keep it short.

## Qualify while you capture

The best part of conversational lead capture is context. By the time someone fills in the form, the transcript already tells you what they care about. Route that to your team along with the lead so the follow-up is personal, not generic.

## Measure what happens next

Watch three things:

- **Conversations started** — is the assistant being used?
- **Leads captured** — how many turn into form submissions?
- **Lead quality** — do they turn into customers?

If conversations are high but leads are low, your ask is mistimed or too heavy. If leads are high but quality is low, tighten your qualifying questions.

## The compounding effect

Unlike a one-off campaign, an AI front desk improves with every transcript you read. You'll spot the questions you didn't anticipate, the objections you can pre-empt, and the language your customers actually use. Feed that back into the knowledge base and the whole thing gets sharper over time.
